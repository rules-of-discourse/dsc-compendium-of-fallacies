Draw from Checkmate malconduct

Malconducts

Basic Description

The Draw from Checkmate is a subtype of Pigeon Chess in which one participant in the discussion (Person A) who is in fact committing the Pidgeon Chess malconduct attempts to extract zirself from the discussion in a way that makes it seem as though zir opponent (Person B) is the one committing the Pidgeon Chess malconduct. An instance of the Draw from Checkmate malconduct is known as a Draw from Chechmate.

The Draw from Checkmate malconduct is named such because when someone commits a Draw from Checkmate, it is analogous to if Person A is playing chess against an oponent (Person B) - and as soon as Person B successfully had checkmated Person A's king, Person A had gotten up and left, claiming that the game was a draw.
