Appeal to Laity Malconduct

Malconducts

Basic Description

The Appeal to Laity Malconduct is when an assertion is presented for the judgement of unqualified masses or for the judgement of some other unqualified yet influential party instead of being submitted to the review of those who are qualified to make a competent assessment of the assertion, or worse, upon having been rejected by the consensus of those who are actually qualified to assess the assertion.

Typically, the unqualifed arbiter that this malconduct is directed at is the general public -- however, the definition of this malconduct does not require that it be the general public.

Typically, those committing this malconduct claim that the fair and equitable solution to a dispute is to allow the public to decide on the matter. However, that claim is deceptive. In reality, this malconduct has the effect of putting claims that have not been tested by relevant experts (or worse, have failed such tests) on the same level as claims that have been exhaustively tested and validated to the point that the overwhelming consensus of qualified experts accept the claim.

Notable Examples

The most notable example of this malconduct is the collection of efforts by proponents of Intelligent Design to have their notion of natural history taught in schools alongside the Theory of Evolution as an "alternative theory" - even though relevant experts overwhelmingly conclude that their ideas lack the criteria needed to even properly qualify as a hypothesis.

Razors

Step 1: START HERE
Ask yourself whether or not the party to whom the evidence is presented is equipped (intellectually and otherwise) to properly and objectively analyze the evidence and/or argument being presented.
If the answer is "yes"
You are then in the clear. This malconduct has not been committed - nor has any other fallacy or malconduct been committed on this count.
If the answer is "no"
Proceed to Step 2.
Step 2
Does there exist an authoritative body that has the necessary qualifications to evaluate the evidence and/or argument provided?
If the answer is "yes"
Yes - this malconduct has been committed or is being committed on this count. Follow proper corrective measures.
If the answer is "no"
Technically, this count isn't an instance of this malconduct. However, the party to which the evidence and/or argument is presented, lacking the qualifications, should refrain from coming to any conclusion.

Granted - this algorithm raises the question at times as to whether or not a specific party has the necessary qualifications. Eventually, a section will be added to this compendium to address that.

Recommended Corrective Measures

If you are the party to whom an Appeal to Laity is being directed, or a member of that party, the correct response is to call out the malconduct and explain who the assertion ought to be presented to. If your idea of who the assertion ought to be presented to is specific, then state it specifically - however, if you only have a very vague idea who the assertion ought to be presented to, then at least state that.

You should never make an assessment on an assertion that you are not qualified to assess - and you should similarly discourage others from making such unqualified assessments.

If you are the gatekeeper of a medium that one is attempting to use as the vehicle of an Appeal to Laity, you also should direct the one attempting to use your medium to instead direct their assertion to the proper body of experts.
