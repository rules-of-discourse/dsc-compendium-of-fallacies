Tone Policing Malconduct

Malconducts

Basic Description

This malconduct is committed when relevant and potentially sound argument is silenced on the basis of real or alleged lack of decorum in the presentation of said argumentation. It is a subtype of the Civility Malconduct.

Subtleties

The Tone Policing malconduct is a very complex malconduct. For this reason, developing systematic razors for distinguishing it from it's faux forms. That said, until such razors are developed, it is nonetheless necessary to provide what guidelines can be provided for such distinction - as it is necessary to prevent provisions against this malconduct from being abused by promoters of truly illegitimate argument without allowing bona-fide cases of this malconduct to slip through the cracks.

As such, the only thing that can be done is to describe the malconduct, as well as the dynamic that it is part of, as clearly as possible - in hopes that such a description can be used as a standard against which cases suspected of this malconduct can be tested.
