Tone Policing Malconduct

Malconducts

Basic Description

This malconduct is committed when relevant and potentially sound argument is silenced on the basis of real or alleged lack of decorum in the presentation of said argumentation. It is a subtype of the Civility Malconduct.

Razors Needed

Razors are essential in defining this malconduct, as abence of proper razors elevate the risk of either (a) failure to adequately prevent this malconduct or (b) abuse of precautions against this malconduct by promoters of illegitimate argument that truly violates sound discourse and rightly should be halted.

That said - the desining of such razors is easier said than done - as this malconduct is an extremely complex one. The following Razors should therefore be regarded as being in early development.
