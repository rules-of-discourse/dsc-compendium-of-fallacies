Appeal to Consequences Fallacy

Fallacies

Basic Description

The Appeal to Consequences fallacy is when one proposition is rejected because of what it would imply - even though what it would imply hasn't been properly ruled out.

Razors

Step 1
Is the person suspected of committing the fallacy Person A claiming that one proposition Proposition B can not be true because it would mean that a certain other proposition Proposition C must also be true?
If the answer is "yes"
Proceed to Step 2
If the answer is "no"
In the clear. This argument doesn't even have the most rudimentary form of the fallacy in question.
Step 2
Has it been properly established that Proposition C is not true?
If the answer is "yes"
In the clear. This is the cogent form of an inverse proof.
If the answer is "no"
Guilty as charged.
