Civility Malconduct

Malconducts

Basic Description

The Civility Malconduct is when a rule of social etiquette or some other standard of civility is enforced at the expense of the integrity of the discourse itself.

This malconduct does not apply to all enforcement of standards of decorum. It merely applies to cases in which such enforcement is done at the expense of the integrity to discourse itself or with disregard as to whether or not it could obstruct discourse. Politeness and civility are both good things. They merely should not be prioritized over integrity of discourse.

This malconduct applies whether the enforcement is done by means of administrative authority, peer pressure, or any other means of enforcement.

Some forms of this malconduct

One clear form of this malconduct is if a group, when formulating and/or enforcing a rule against personal attacks, fails to provide an exception in the case of personal attacks that are logically relevant to the discussion at hand.

Other forms of this malconduct may be a little more complex and far more nuanced than the formentioned form.
