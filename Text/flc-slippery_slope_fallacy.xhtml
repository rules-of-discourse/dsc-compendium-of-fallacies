Slippery Slope Fallacy

Fallacies

Basic Description

The Slippery Slope fallacy is when a course of action (or inaction) is objected to on the grounds that it would lead to a specific result despite the lack of a sound basis for the conclusion that it really would lead to that undesired result.

Razors

Step 1
Is the argument that a particular course of action or inaction Course A should not be used because it would lead to a particular result Situation B?
If the answer is "yes"
Proceed to Step 2.
If the answer is "no"
As far as this particular fallacy is concerned, the argument is in the clear.
Step 2
Do reason and evidence support the conclusion that Course A would result in Situation B?
If the answer is "yes"
The argument is in the clear.
If the answer is "no"
In that case, yes, this is indeed a case of the Slippery Slope fallacy.
