Straw Man Fallacy

Fallacies

Basic Description

The Straw Man Fallacy is committed by someone who instead of addressing the actual argument or claim that another made, instead addresses a caricature of that argument.

The Straw Man Fallacy can be divided between the Lesser Straw Man Fallacy and the Classic Straw Man Fallacy.

The Lesser Straw Man Fallacy is when someone is reckless in their assumption about the other's argument. Their guess about the other's argument may be correct, but if that's so, it's due to dumb luck, rather than sound reasoning on their own part.

The Classic Straw Man Fallacy is a deliberate distortion of the other's arguments. It is named "Classic" because it is closest to the Straw Man Fallacy as described in traditional sources.

Razors

To determine whether or not the person suspected of committing the Straw Man Fallacy Person A did in fact do so:

Step 1
Does Person A present as knowing for fact that the argument or claim that zie is addressing Justification C is indeed the one made by the other Party B?
If the answer is "yes"
Proceed to Step 2
If the answer is "no"
Person A is in the clear. This is a hypothetical reputation - and at least on this particular account, cogent.
Step 2
Has Person A verified whether or not Justification C is indeed the argument or claim made by Party B?
If the answer is "yes"
Proceed to Step 3
If the answer is "no"
This is a case of the Lesser Straw Man Fallacy.
Step 3
Is Justification C indeed the argument or claim made by Party B?
If the answer is "yes"
In the clear
If the answer is "no"
This is a case of the Classic Straw Man Fallacy.
