The Malconducts

The malconducts are conducts or behaviors that are anticonducive to proper discourse, and which the Rules of Discourse therefore instruct participants to avoid.

A malconduct is different from a fallacy in that a fallacy is a pattern of argument while a malconduct is a pattern of action. The use of a fallacy automatically qualifies as a malconduct, however there are some malconducts for which a fallacy can not be specifically named.

Traditional compendia of fallacies do not contain specific sections for malconducts. This is problematic. This has resulted in some malconducts (such as the Appeal to Force Malconduct) being listed as fallacies when in reality they are more accurately classified as malconducts. It has also resulted in other malconducts such as the Homework Malconduct being left out altogether.
